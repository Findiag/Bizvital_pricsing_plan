① Financial Diagnostic — what each module means to you
1.1 Growth
Starter ✓
Is my business actually getting bigger?
Revenue is up this month — but is that real growth or a one-off order? The Growth module strips out seasonal noise and one-time spikes to show you whether your business is on a genuine upward trajectory or just having a lucky month....
Real question answered: "Should I hire more people or wait?" — Growth score below 60 says wait. Above 75 says you have room to expand.
1.2 Profitability
Starter ✓
Am I actually making money, or just busy?
The most common shock for Indian business owners: "My revenue is ₹2 crore but I have nothing in the bank." Profitability shows exactly where money is earned vs where it disappears — from gross margin all the way to what you actually keep....
Real question answered: "Why does my profit look good on paper but my bank account feels empty?" — This module tells you exactly why.
1.3 Efficiency
Starter ✓
Am I spending ₹1 to earn ₹1, or ₹1 to earn ₹3?
Efficiency tells you how well your business converts effort and cost into output. A low efficiency score doesn't mean your team is lazy — it usually means your processes, pricing, or cost structure has a hidden leak that's silently eating your margins....
Real question answered: "Are we working harder this year but making less?" — Efficiency score trending down while revenue goes up is the first warning sign.
1.4 Turnover
Growth ✓
Is my money sitting still or working hard?
You have stock sitting in a warehouse. You have invoices waiting to be paid....
Real question answered: "I bought ₹50L worth of stock. How long before I see that money back?" — Turnover score gives you the answer in plain numbers.
1.5 Business Risk
Growth ✓
What could shut my business down that I'm not seeing?
Customer concentration (2 clients = 70% revenue), supplier dependency, revenue seasonality, geographic risk — these are the silent landmines in every growing business. Business Risk scans for them before they become emergencies....
Real question answered: "If my top customer leaves tomorrow, do I survive?" — Business Risk score below 50 means the honest answer is probably no. You need to know that.
1.6 Financial Risk
Growth ✓
Am I borrowing too much? Can I handle another bad month?
Most businesses don't fail because of bad sales — they fail because they took on too much debt relative to what they earn. Financial Risk measures your leverage, your interest coverage, and your ability to absorb a shock — like a delayed customer payment or a sudden cost spike....
Real question answered: "Should I take that ₹50L business loan?" — Financial Risk score tells you if your business can actually handle more debt right now.
1.7 Liquidity
Growth ✓
Will I run out of cash before my next payment comes in?
The most terrifying feeling in business: salary day is in 10 days, a big customer payment is delayed by 30 days, and you have ₹3L in the bank. Liquidity tracks how much breathing room you have between your cash inflows and outflows at any point in time....
Real question answered: "Do I have enough cash to run the business for the next 60 days even if nothing goes right?" — Liquidity score answers this every single week.
1.8 Safety
Growth ✓
Is my business fundamentally stable or walking a tightrope?
Safety is the big-picture solvency check. Are your assets worth more than your liabilities?...
Real question answered: "If I wanted to raise funding or take a bank loan today, would my business qualify?" — Safety score tells you the honest answer before you walk into that meeting.
② Cross-Functional Diagnostic — why knowing your departments matters
The problem no accounting tool solves
Your P&L tells you the business made ₹12L profit this quarter. But it doesn't tell you that your Sales team is performing brilliantly while your Operations department is quietly burning ₹4L in waste every month. Without department-level visibility, you're managing the whole business by looking at one number.
2.1 Sales
Starter ✓
Is my sales team actually performing or just busy?
Tracks revenue growth, deal conversion efficiency, customer acquisition cost, and concentration risk. Your team can be running 50 meetings a month and still have a poor Sales score if those meetings aren't converting profitably.
Tells you: Which customers are worth keeping, which aren't, and whether your sales effort is producing real margin.
2.2 Finance
Starter ✓
Is the finance function helping or hiding problems?
Tracks collection efficiency, payment cycles, working capital management, and cost control. A poor Finance dept score often reveals slow collections dragging your cash position — even when the business is selling well.
Tells you: Whether your collections team is chasing payments fast enough and if your cash conversion is as fast as it should be.
2.3 Management
Growth ✓
Are my managers running a tight ship or a leaky one?
Measures overhead cost control, decision-making efficiency, budget adherence, and admin cost as a % of revenue. Many growing businesses see management costs silently balloon as they scale — this catches it early.
Tells you: Whether your management layer is adding value relative to what it costs — a conversation most founders avoid.
2.4 Material
Growth ✓
Am I overpaying for what I buy, or leaving money on the table?
For any business buying raw materials, inventory, or supplies — Material tracks procurement costs, vendor payment terms, inventory turnover, and material cost as % of revenue. One bad vendor relationship can quietly destroy your margin.
Tells you: Which vendors are costing you more than they should and whether your purchase costs are trending in the right direction.
2.5 Operations
Growth ✓
Is my business running smoothly or is something always on fire?
Tracks operational cost structure, output efficiency, fixed vs variable cost balance, and capacity utilisation. Operations is often where the biggest hidden costs live — especially in manufacturing, distribution, and service businesses.
Tells you: Whether you're running at a cost structure that can scale — or one that will break you as you grow.
2.6 HR
Growth ✓
Am I getting value from my people cost?
Payroll is usually the single largest cost in a service or knowledge business. HR tracks revenue per employee, salary cost as % of revenue, attrition cost impact, and productivity trends. A low HR score doesn't mean your team is bad — it often means your hiring is ahead of your revenue.
Tells you: Whether you've over-hired, under-hired, or whether your team cost is eating into the profit that growth should be delivering.
Analysis Tools — turning data into decisions
③
Impact Analysis — "What happens if I change X?"
Before you increase prices, add a new product line, or change a vendor — Impact Analysis shows you the downstream financial effect across your entire business. It's the answer to "I have an idea, but I don't know if it'll actually make things better."
Example: "If I increase my product price by 8%, does my net margin go up or do I lose enough customers to make it worse?"
④
Risk Analysis — "What are the things that could hurt me?"
Maps every material risk in your business — customer concentration, market dependency, cost volatility, cash cycle gaps — and ranks them by financial impact. Most business owners only discover their risks when they become crises.
Example: "My top 3 customers are 65% of revenue. Risk Analysis flags this as critical and shows you exactly what to do to diversify."
⑤
What-if Analysis — "Let me test this idea before I commit"
Run scenarios against your real financial data. Hire 5 people. Open a new branch. Cut a product line. What-if Analysis runs each scenario and shows you the projected impact on cash, profit, and risk — before you spend a single rupee.
Example: "What if I hire 3 senior salespeople at ₹1.2L/month each — how long before they pay for themselves given my current conversion rate?"
⑥
Profit Leakage — "Show me the money I'm losing without knowing"
This is Bizvitals' most powerful feature. Every business leaks profit in ways that never show up clearly in a P&L — underpriced products, slow collections, vendor discounts not claimed, overtime costs creeping up. Profit Leakage finds them all and puts a ₹ figure on each.
The average Indian SMB has 8–15% of revenue in recoverable profit leakage. On ₹1Cr revenue that's ₹8–15L/year sitting on the table.
⑦
Heat Map — "Show me the problem at a glance"
A visual colour map of your entire business health — green is good, red needs attention, yellow is a warning. For business owners who think visually rather than numerically, the Heat Map is often the first thing they look at every Monday morning. One glance and you know where to spend your attention this week.
Example: Three greens, one yellow (Operations), one red (Liquidity). You now know exactly what deserves your attention today — without reading a single number.
