The 20-minute
CXO demo script

For business owners and CXOs who don't understand finance. The goal is not to impress — it's to make them feel seen, then show them something that surprises them.

Demo length
20 mins
Strict. Respect their time.
Target persona
Owner / CXO
Non-finance decision maker
Demo data
Industry match
Have 3 industry datasets ready
Demo goal
Trial or Growth
Close on the call if possible
Phase 1 — Discovery0–4 minutes · Listen, don't pitch
1
The opening question — never start with your product
0:00 – 1:30
"Before I show you anything — tell me, when your CA or accountant sends you the monthly P&L, what's the one thing you always end up asking them to explain?"
This confirms they have a CA, surfaces their biggest pain, and makes them feel heard. Write down their answer visibly — you will reference it word-for-word at the close.
Never open with "let me give you a quick overview of our platform." That kills calls in the first 30 seconds.
2
The pain qualifier
1:30 – 3:30
"How often do you actually look at your financial reports? And when you do — honestly — do you feel like you fully understand what they're telling you, or does it feel like reading something in a foreign language?"
Most will laugh and say "foreign language, honestly." That's your cue.
"That's exactly what we built Bizvitals for. Most financial software is built for CAs and accountants. We built this for you — the person actually running the business."
"Built for you, not your CA" is your core positioning. Say it early, say it clearly. This separates Bizvitals from every accounting tool they've seen.
3
Set the demo agenda — give them control
3:30 – 4:00
"I have about 15 minutes to show you three things. First, your complete financial health score in plain language. Second, how each of your departments is actually performing — Sales, Finance, Operations, and more. Third, where money might be leaking from your business right now. Sound good?"
Mentioning the department diagnostics upfront is new — it sets up the cross-functional demo as a core product promise, not an add-on.
Phase 2 — The Demo4–16 minutes · Show don't tell. Always use their industry data.
4
Financial health score — the first aha moment
4:00 – 7:00
Open a demo account preloaded with their industry data. Show the dashboard. Silence for 3 seconds before speaking.
"One score — your overall financial health. But the real value is drilling in. See this — Profitability is at 61. Sounds okay. But look at the breakdown — gross margin is fine, but net margin is being crushed by operating costs. In plain English: you're selling well, but something is eating your profits before it reaches you."
Always translate every score into a plain English sentence immediately. That's the entire product promise. Practice this for all 8 modules until it's natural.
5
Cross-functional department diagnostic — the differentiator
7:00 – 10:00
"Now here's something you won't find in Tally or Zoho. We don't just tell you about your finances — we show you how each department in your business is performing."
Navigate to the Cross-Functional Diagnostic. Show the 6 department tiles — Sales, Finance, Management, Material, Operations, HR — each with a score. Let them see it before you explain.
"Each of these is a health score for that department. So if your Sales score is 72 but your Operations score is 48 — you know exactly where the problem is. No more guessing. No more waiting for your CA to tell you something you already felt was wrong."
"How does it calculate the department score?"
"It analyses the financial indicators that map to each department — for Sales it's revenue growth, collection efficiency, customer concentration risk. For Operations it's cost structure, inventory turn, and output efficiency. All pulled from your existing data."
This is your biggest differentiator moment. No competitor shows department-level financial health in plain language. Slow down here. Let the 6 department scores breathe — a business owner seeing their HR or Operations score for the first time is a powerful moment.
6
Profit Leakage — the money shot
10:00 – 13:00
"Now let me show you the feature that usually makes people sit forward in their chair."
Navigate to Profit Leakage. Pause 3 full seconds. Let them read the number before you speak.
"This business has ₹1.8 lakh per month in identified profit leakage — hiding in pricing inconsistencies, collection delays, and vendor payment timing. Each has a fix. And notice — two of these leakages trace back directly to the Operations and Finance departments we just looked at. This is why the department view matters."
Connect the Profit Leakage back to the departments you just showed. This makes the product feel like a complete system, not separate features. The cross-functional view suddenly feels essential, not optional.
Don't explain the algorithm. They care about the ₹ number and which department it traces to.
7
AI Observations — the closer
13:00 – 15:30
"The last thing — your AI CFO. Instead of a report full of numbers, it talks to you in plain language."
Open AI Observations. Read one out loud — slowly — like a CA giving personal advice.
"'Your Operations department cost structure has deteriorated 11% over the last quarter, contributing to the profit leakage identified above. Recommend reviewing vendor contracts and overhead allocation before Q3.' — A CA would charge ₹5,000 to write that. You get it automatically, every week, about every department."
The CA comparison is powerful in India. And now you've tied it to the department view — the AI observation references a specific department, which makes the whole system feel deeply connected.
Phase 3 — The Close16–20 minutes · Ask once, then be quiet
8
The tie-back close — use their exact words from step 1
16:00 – 17:30
"At the start you mentioned [their exact words]. Did you see how the Liquidity module, the Operations department score, and the AI observation all address exactly that? That's not a coincidence — that's what Bizvitals is built to surface."
Tie the close to their words AND to the department diagnostic. If they run a business with multiple departments, the cross-functional view is often what converts them — "finally I can see every part of my business in one place."
9
The pricing reveal — anchor high, land on Growth
17:30 – 19:00
"We have three plans. Most business owners start on Growth — ₹999 a month, less than a single CA consultation. You get all 8 financial modules, all 6 department diagnostics, the Profit Leakage scan, and the AI observations every week."
Lead with Growth. Say "all 6 department diagnostics" prominently — that's the feature that most CXOs won't get anywhere else and it lands strongly in the pricing pitch.
Don't mention Starter first. Don't list every single feature. Show the pricing page, point to Growth, stop talking.
10
The ask — direct and calm
19:00 – 20:00
"Would you like to start the 14-day trial today? No card needed, and I'll personally make sure your first department diagnostic report is set up correctly."
After the ask — stop talking. The next person who speaks loses. Silence after a close is uncomfortable but it works. Don't say "no pressure" — it signals you don't believe in the product.
Objection HandlerThe 4 you will definitely hear
"I already have a CA / accountant. Why do I need this?"
"Your CA is great at compliance and tax. Bizvitals is for you — to understand your business between CA meetings, including how each of your departments is actually performing. Think of it as your CA's monthly report, translated into plain English, available every day."
"We already use Tally / Zoho / Excel."
"Those record your transactions. Bizvitals reads those transactions and tells you what they mean — across your overall financials AND across every department. Your Sales team's performance, your Operations cost structure, your HR efficiency — all in one view. They're inputs to us, not competitors."
"₹999 seems expensive for a new product."
"Completely fair — 14-day free trial, no card needed. But think about it: if the Profit Leakage feature finds ₹10,000 in one month, you've paid for the tool 10 times over. And if one department diagnostic reveals a cost problem you didn't know existed — that's potentially lakhs saved."
"I need to show this to my partner / CFO / CA first."
"Absolutely — start the trial today and I'll generate a one-page summary including your department health scores. That way they see real data from your business, not just a brochure. Department scores are usually the most convincing thing to show a co-founder or CFO."
The Bizvitals one-liner — memorise this
"We translate your financial data into plain language — including how every department in your business is performing — so you can run your company without needing a finance degree."
Use when someone asks "what does Bizvitals do?" — at a networking event, on a call, in a WhatsApp message. The department line is what makes it different from every other finance tool they've heard of.
